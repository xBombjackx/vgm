CONDITIONAL INTEREST - Major Revisions Required

Editorial Strengths

The manuscript addresses a genuine scholarly gap. Our peer reviewers in both musicology and game studies confirmed that no comprehensive academic treatment of Japanese game music hardware exists in English. The "Sound Programmer as Auteur" thesis is particularly compelling and would position this within our Oxford Music/Media series alongside Sumanth Gopinath's The Ringtone Dialectic.

The technical depth is impressive. Unlike Karen Collins' work (which we also publish), this manuscript doesn't shy away from engineering specifics. The YM2612 Ladder Effect analysis demonstrates the author understands that hardware archaeology is musicological archaeology.

Critical Weaknesses

  • Audience confusion: The manuscript attempts to serve both academic researchers and contemporary producers. For OUP, this is a fatal flaw. We publish for scholars and students, not for prosumer musicians seeking production tips.
  • Citation rigor: The bibliography includes Reddit threads, YouTube videos, and forum posts. Our peer review process requires 70%+ scholarly sources (peer-reviewed journals, academic monographs, primary archival materials).
  • Theoretical underdevelopment: The manuscript jumps directly into hardware analysis without establishing a robust theoretical framework. Where is the engagement with sound studies (Sterne, Pinch & Bijsterveld), platform studies (Bogost & Montfort), or media archaeology (Ernst, Parikka)?
  • Geographic limitation: Focusing exclusively on Japanese production limits the book's appeal. Our most successful titles in this space (e.g., Collins' From Pac-Man to Pop Music) take global comparative approaches.

Required Changes for Acceptance

  • Delete Chapters 10-11 (Tracker/DAW workflows). Software tutorials date within 18 months; our books remain in print for 10+ years.
  • Add Introduction chapter (15,000-20,000 words) with comprehensive literature review, theoretical positioning, and methodology explanation.
  • Add comparative Western chapter: Contrast Japanese FM synthesis aesthetics with Western sample-based approaches (Amiga MOD scene, Soundblaster era). This broadens market appeal to North American university courses.
  • Conduct primary research: The manuscript currently relies on secondary sources. We require original contribution—this means archival work (consulting Sega/Nintendo corporate archives), oral histories (interviewing sound programmers in Japanese), or computational analysis (MIR datasets proving Jazz Fusion harmonic influence).
  • Rebuild bibliography: Minimum 200 sources, 70% peer-reviewed. Replace YouTube/Reddit citations with primary sources or eliminate those claims.
  • Add 40-60 musical examples: Staff notation, reduction scores, spectrographic analysis. We have world-class music engraving capabilities—use them.
